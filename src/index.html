The story of Tomatín.

The story of Tomatín.

Long time ago in a distant vegetable garden, a little tomato fell off his mother plant. At that moment, he knew, he would be on his own, away from the protection of his mother and far from his brothers and sisters.

Facing the world by himself was going to be challenging, but he was determined. After several months, he got a job as one of the seed keepers in the garden. All he had to do was selecting the best seeds for the garden and count them so other workers could store them for the next season.

He did a good job. He was good at it but Tomatín, as everyone used to call him, knew he could do better and he started investigating how to become a more productive worker.

So months later, our little friend realized that, after some time working hard, he was no longer focused; at least not as much as the beginning of the day. His mind needed a break from time to time or his work would worsen. So he thought: «I should take a break every now and then so my mind keeps focused. Even if it is only 5 minutes». Eureka!

He started developing a new technique for work. He would work completely focused for several minutes and then he would take a short break before continuing with another session of work. He finally decided that 25 minutes was the perfect duration for each session, working fully focused, before taking the five minutes break. He even determined that having a longer break, of about 15 minutes, after a cycle of 4 sessions would make him be even more productive.

And so he did this for many years. And this tiny change in his life made him the greatest and most efficient seed keeper of all time.

Since then, Tomatín would always take time seriously.

Years later, he had a chat about his technique with a friend, Francesco Cirillo, who then popularized his technique, naming it Pomodoro as a tribute to the little tomato who takes time seriously.
